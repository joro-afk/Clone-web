  • Crafting a fictional world by representing the best parts of this one.

    Speculative fiction often turns the lens back on the reader, making the previously unknown become knowable by turning it into story. It’s long been used as a vehicle for representation, from Star Trek exploring new frontiers of romance, to Octavia Butler positioning a Black woman at the forefront of humanity’s escape to space in Parable of the Sower, to dystopian YA like The Hunger Games tapping into every young woman’s desire to change the world by burning it down.

    Lindsay Ishihiro

    Writer, I Was a Teenage Exocolonist
